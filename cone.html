Ritangle Question 11: Solution Guide

The Goal: Find the sum of the possible values of \( a \) by calculating the volumes of two frustums generated by rotating a line segment, setting their volume difference to 50.

Step 1: Understanding the Geometry

A frustum is the portion of a cone that remains after its upper part has been cut off. The formula for the volume of a frustum is:

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \pi h (R^2 + r^2 + Rr)$$

The line segment connects \( P_1(1, 2) \) and \( P_2(12, a) \). Here is a visualization of the segment on the XY plane:

Figure 1: The line segment connecting (1, 2) and (12, a).

Step 2: Rotation about the x-axis (\( V_x \))

When rotating around the x-axis, the object formed is a frustum lying on its side.

  • Height (\( h_x \)): \( 12 - 1 = 11 \)
  • Radii (\( r_x, R_x \)): \( 2 \) and \( a \) (vertical distances from x-axis)
$$V_x = \frac{11\pi}{3} (a^2 + 2a + 4)$$

Figure 2: Rotation about the x-axis.

Step 3: Rotation about the y-axis (\( V_y \))

When rotating around the y-axis, the object is an upright bucket shape.

  • Height (\( h_y \)): \( a - 2 \)
  • Radii (\( r_y, R_y \)): \( 1 \) and \( 12 \) (horizontal distances from y-axis)
$$V_y = \frac{157\pi}{3} (a - 2)$$

Figure 3: Rotation about the y-axis.

Step 4 & 5: Equation & Solution

We set the absolute difference of the volumes to 50: \(|V_x - V_y| = 50\).

This results in two quadratic equations. We calculate the sum of roots for both cases:

  • Sum for Case A: \( \frac{135}{11} \)
  • Sum for Case B: \( \frac{135}{11} \)

Adding these together gives the total sum:

$$\text{Total Sum} = \frac{135}{11} + \frac{135}{11} = \frac{270}{11}$$
Final Answer: \( \frac{270}{11} \approx 24.545 \)
