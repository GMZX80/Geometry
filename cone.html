Ritangle Question 11: Solution Guide

The Goal: Find the sum of the possible values of \( a \) by calculating the volumes of two frustums generated by rotating a line segment, setting their volume difference to 50.

Step 1: Understanding the Geometry

A frustum is the portion of a cone that remains after its upper part has been cut off. The formula for the volume of a frustum is:

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \pi h (R^2 + r^2 + Rr)$$

Where:

  • \( h \) = height (distance along the axis of rotation)
  • \( R \) = radius of the large base
  • \( r \) = radius of the small base

The line segment connects \( P_1(1, 2) \) and \( P_2(12, a) \), where \( a > 2 \).

Figure 1: The line segment to be rotated.

Step 2: Rotation about the x-axis (\( V_x \))

When rotating around the x-axis, the height is the horizontal distance, and the radii are the vertical distances (y-values).

  • Height (\( h_x \)): \( 12 - 1 = 11 \)
  • Radii (\( r_x, R_x \)): \( 2 \) and \( a \)

Substituting into the formula:

$$V_x = \frac{1}{3} \pi (11) (a^2 + 2^2 + 2a)$$ $$V_x = \frac{11\pi}{3} (a^2 + 2a + 4)$$

Figure 2: The frustum formed by x-axis rotation.

Step 3: Rotation about the y-axis (\( V_y \))

When rotating around the y-axis, the height is the vertical distance, and the radii are the horizontal distances (x-values).

  • Height (\( h_y \)): \( a - 2 \) (since \( a > 2 \))
  • Radii (\( r_y, R_y \)): \( 1 \) and \( 12 \)

Substituting into the formula:

$$V_y = \frac{1}{3} \pi (a - 2) (12^2 + 1^2 + 12 \cdot 1)$$ $$V_y = \frac{157\pi}{3} (a - 2)$$

Figure 3: The frustum formed by y-axis rotation.

Step 4: Setting up the Equation

The problem states the difference in volumes is 50. We must consider the absolute difference:

$$|V_x - V_y| = 50$$

Case A: \( V_x - V_y = 50 \)

$$11a^2 - 135a + \left(358 - \frac{150}{\pi}\right) = 0$$

Case B: \( V_y - V_x = 50 \)

$$11a^2 - 135a + \left(358 + \frac{150}{\pi}\right) = 0$$

Step 5: Solving for the Sum

For a quadratic equation \( Ax^2 + Bx + C = 0 \), the sum of the roots is \( \frac{-B}{A} \).

  • Sum for Case A: \( \frac{135}{11} \)
  • Sum for Case B: \( \frac{135}{11} \)

The total sum of all possible values for \( a \) is:

$$\text{Total Sum} = \frac{135}{11} + \frac{135}{11} = \frac{270}{11}$$
Final Answer: \( \frac{270}{11} \approx 24.545 \)
